Buying licenses is not a rollout. Real adoption is a land-and-expand motion: prove value somewhere specific, then make success repeatable. Here's the playbook.

## Phase 1 — Discover

Understand the terrain before you spend political capital.

- **Find the pain.** Where does delivery actually slow down — onboarding, code review, boilerplate, legacy comprehension? Target a pain Cursor plausibly relieves.
- **Map constraints.** Security, compliance, and procurement requirements (data handling, SOC 2, SSO/SCIM). Engage security *now*, not after the pilot. See the [security guide](/learn/security).
- **Pick a lighthouse team.** Choose one team that is motivated, representative, and shipping something visible — not your most heroic team (they'll succeed with anything) and not your most skeptical (you'll fight noise instead of learning).

## Phase 2 — Pilot

Small, instrumented, time-boxed.

- **Configure for success.** Ship a baseline `.cursor/rules` encoding the team's conventions and guardrails, set up any [MCP](/learn/feature-tour#mcp) integrations (least-privilege), and commit a strong `.cursorignore`. Don't make the pilot team discover all this themselves.
- **Pick ONE success metric.** Resist a dashboard of twelve. One primary metric the team agrees on (e.g. PR cycle time) makes the result legible to leadership.
- **Baseline first.** Measure the metric *before* turning Cursor on, so you have an honest comparison.
- **Enable Privacy Mode org-wide** before anyone writes a line — security is a precondition, not a phase-two cleanup.

<Callout type="tip" title="Configuration is the difference">
Teams that get a curated setup — good rules, the right MCP servers, a sane `.cursorignore` — see results fast. Teams handed a raw install often conclude "it didn't help." The tool is the same; the setup isn't.
</Callout>

## Phase 3 — Prove value

Turn the pilot into an argument leadership believes.

- **Tie usage to outcomes.** Connect adoption to the metric you chose and to qualitative signal (developer sentiment, concrete wins). Run it like a QBR: here's the baseline, here's the result, here's what we learned.
- **Be honest about what didn't work.** Credibility compounds. Documenting failure modes makes your expansion plan trustworthy.

### Metrics that matter

| Metric | What it tells you |
| --- | --- |
| **DORA** (deploy frequency, lead time, change-fail rate, MTTR) | Whether speed gains come without stability loss |
| **PR cycle time** | How fast work moves from open to merged |
| **Time-to-first-commit** | How quickly new joiners become productive |

<Callout type="warn" title="Avoid vanity metrics">
"Lines of AI code accepted" or "suggestions shown" measure activity, not value — and reward the wrong behavior. Anchor on delivery and stability outcomes the business already cares about.
</Callout>

## Phase 4 — Expand

Make the lighthouse result repeatable.

- **Build a playbook.** Package what worked: the baseline rules, recommended MCP servers, onboarding steps, and the metric definitions. New teams should start from a template, not a blank slate.
- **Manage change.** Adoption is a people problem. Provide enablement (office hours, internal champions, short guides), set expectations, and let teams adapt the playbook to their context.
- **Expand along the grain.** Move to teams with similar workflows next, where the playbook transfers cleanly, before tackling the outliers.

## Phase 5 — Govern

Sustain it safely at scale.

- **Enforce security centrally:** SSO/SAML/SCIM, audit logs and AI-code tracking, model/provider allow-lists, org-wide Privacy Mode. ([Security guide](/learn/security#enterprise-controls).)
- **Govern spend:** soft spend limits, alerts, and per-user/per-model monitoring — both a budget and a safety control. ([Cost guide](/learn/cost).)
- **Keep the playbook alive.** Conventions and rules should evolve as the org learns. Assign an owner.

## The one-page summary

1. **Discover** the pain and the constraints; pick a lighthouse team.
2. **Pilot** with a curated setup, one metric, and a real baseline.
3. **Prove value** by tying usage to DORA / cycle-time / time-to-first-commit.
4. **Expand** with a repeatable playbook and genuine change management.
5. **Govern** with enforced security and spend controls.

Start your rollout on the right foot: run your baseline config through the **[Config Analyzer](/tools/config-analyzer)** so every team begins from a secure, cost-aware setup.
